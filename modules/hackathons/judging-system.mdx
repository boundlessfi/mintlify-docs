---
title: "Judging system"
description: "How hackathon submissions are judged and winners selected on Boundless."
---

Hackathon submissions on Boundless are evaluated through a configurable judging system. Organizers set criteria and judges; scores determine winners and prize distribution.

## How it works

1. **Criteria** The organizer defines what judges score (e.g., innovation, execution, design, use of theme).
2. **Judges** Designated judges (or the organizer) score each submission.
3. **Scoring** Scores are combined (e.g., average or weighted) to rank submissions.
4. **Winners** Top-ranked projects win prizes according to the event’s prize structure. Funds release from escrow to winners.

## For participants

- Submit before the deadline and ensure your demo/repo meets the event rules.
- Judging is based on the published criteria; no need to do anything extra unless the organizer requests it.

## For organizers

- Set clear, measurable criteria so judges can score consistently.
- Add or assign judges in the event dashboard and define how scores are aggregated.
