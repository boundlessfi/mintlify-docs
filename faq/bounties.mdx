---
title: "FAQ: Bounties"
description: "Frequently asked questions about bounties on Boundless."
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is a bounty?">
    A bounty is a task with a reward. The reward is held in escrow until someone completes the work and is selected according to the bounty’s [claiming model](/modules/bounties/four-claiming-models).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What are the claiming models?">
    There are four main models: first to complete, best submission, split/multiple winners, and custom. See [Four claiming models](/modules/bounties/four-claiming-models).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What is a lightning round?">
    A lightning round is a time-limited window during which claimers can submit. After it ends, no new submissions are accepted. See [Lightning rounds](/modules/bounties/lightning-rounds).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I get paid for a bounty?">
    When you’re selected as the winner (or one of the winners), the reward is released from escrow to your Boundless wallet. You can hold XLM, use it on the platform, or [off-ramp](/concepts/off-ramping) where available.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I disagree with a bounty outcome?">
    You can open a dispute and provide evidence. See [Handle disputes](/how-to-guides/handle-disputes).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
